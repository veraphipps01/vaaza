Anastasia Black

Ceramic artist, Sculptor

Was born in 1987 in Kronstadt, a restricted naval base near Saint Petersburg, Russia. She learned classical photography from the Soviet filmmaker Valery Samarin at the Moscow Aviation Institute, exploring natural forms and black-and-white contrasts which influence many of her works. In 2018, Anastasia emigrated to the United States, where she now lives and practices art in Los Angeles, CA.

Emigration experience and the immersion in both Russian and American cultures have become an impetus for the pursuit of her own sincere artistic dialect, calling on inspiration from native folklore and Russian womenâ€™s status in everyday life. The main theme of this personal and artistic style since childhood in the USSR and later in Russia is the phenomenon of happiness and the ability to be happy, as well as the critical attitude of the image of a poor artist, common for Russian culture.

Hence, ceramics and sculpture have a sense of happiness and satisfaction at the heart of the process of their creation and become mediums for communicating and sharing happy moments with others through art. Clay is employed as an anthropomorphic material open to tactile contact and allowing one to build your own personality, as well as fix the traces of this work for both the artist and the viewer.
